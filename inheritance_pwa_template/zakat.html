📚 Zakat Information

What is Zakat?

Zakat is an annual charitable payment that is obligatory for Muslims who meet the nisab (minimum threshold). It is calculated as 2.5% of your total wealth and is distributed to those in need.

Nisab Threshold

The nisab is equivalent to the value of 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver. You are only required to pay Zakat if your wealth exceeds this threshold for a full lunar year.

What is Included?

  • • Cash and bank accounts
  • • Gold and silver (jewelry, coins, bars)
  • • Investments and stocks
  • • Business inventory and assets
  • • Accounts receivable

What is Excluded?

  • • Primary residence
  • • Personal vehicles
  • • Business equipment (if used)
  • • Debts and liabilities
  • • Wealth below nisab
